Volume vs Weight

When converting 0.25 cup to grams, the core issue is the difference between volume and weight. A cup measures the amount of space an ingredient occupies, while grams measure how heavy that ingredient is. Two ingredients can occupy the same volume but weigh very different amounts. Flour is light and airy, while sugar crystals are dense and heavy. This is why a quarter cup of flour weighs far less than a quarter cup of sugar, even though both fill the same measuring cup.

Density and Particle Structure

Density is influenced by particle size, shape, and moisture content. Finely milled powders like flour trap air between particles, lowering their overall weight per cup. Granulated sugar packs more efficiently, leaving less air space. Brown sugar contains moisture and is often packed intentionally, increasing weight further. Human Measuring Behavior

Human measuring behavior introduces variation even when using the same ingredient. Scooping flour directly from a bag compresses it, while spooning flour gently into a cup introduces more air. Tapping the cup, leveling aggressively, or sifting all change the final weight. These small actions explain why two people can measure the same 0.25 cup and still get different gram values.
